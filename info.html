The Not-So-Long History......

Development

Under the guidance of Josef Fares, the director behind "A Way Out" and "Brothers: A Tale of Two Sons," a team of 60 individuals at Hazelight Studios collaborated to develop "It Takes Two." Following the success of "A Way Out" in 2018, the team decided to embark on another cooperative-only game, leveraging their expertise to further enhance and expand the gameplay ideas explored in their previous creation. The development team placed significant emphasis on weaving a seamless connection between the gameplay and the narrative, ensuring that the mechanics evolved alongside the unfolding story. Josef Fares urged the inclusion of a plethora of mechanics and setpieces, driven by his belief that repetition of gameplay elements diminishes their uniqueness. With the director describing the game as a "romantic comedy," Josef Fares himself contributed to the motion capture for a pivotal character, Dr. Hakim. The majority of the game's code was penned in AngelScript, skillfully integrated into the Unreal Engine through a proprietary plugin developed by Hazelight Studios.

Plot

"It Takes Two" follows Cody and May, a couple planning to divorce, who get transformed into dolls by their daughter Rose. Guided by Dr. Hakim, they embark on a journey to restore their relationship and return to their human bodies. Along the way, they rediscover their love through collaborative challenges and reminisce about cherished memories. Meanwhile, Rose tries to mend their relationship but feels responsible for their issues and runs away. After completing Dr. Hakim's tests, Cody and May reunite with Rose, reassuring her of their unconditional love. The trio returns home with a renewed perspective on their bond.

How to play

Cooperate and Communicate
Collaborate with your partner throughout the game, as each level requires both players' active participation. Communicate effectively to coordinate actions and solve puzzles together.
Utilize Character Abilities
Make use of Cody and May's distinct abilities, such as Cody's ability to control time and May's ability to grow and shrink objects. Strategically deploy these abilities to progress through challenges.
Embrace the Story
Immerse yourself in the touching narrative as it unfolds. Pay attention to the character development and embrace the emotional journey of the couple as they rediscover their love.
Overcome Challenges
Face various obstacles and challenges set by Dr. Hakim. Work as a team to overcome these hurdles and strengthen Cody and May's bond.
Explore Creative Environments
Enjoy exploring diverse and imaginative environments that reflect the couple's experiences and emotions.
Enjoy the Journey
Embrace the spirit of cooperation, discovery, and emotional storytelling as you play "It Takes Two" together with your partner.

Tips and Strategies

Wall Jumping for Height
When struggling to reach higher platforms or solve puzzles, players can gain extra height by continuously jumping against nearby walls.
Color Coordination
Pay attention to colors in the game. Yellow indicates the next objective, green is favorable, and red signifies potential dangers.
Dual Puzzles
Puzzles will often require both players to perform different tasks simultaneously. Always be proactive and look for tasks nearby when your partner is engaged in one.
Crouching for Solutions
Crouching is essential for solving various puzzles, allowing players to access hidden areas or retrieve objects required for progress.
Discover Boss Weaknesses
Each boss has unique weaknesses. Players must observe carefully to find and exploit these weaknesses during boss battles.
Effective Coordination
As a cooperative game, players must communicate and coordinate effectively to make progress smoothly.
Resurrecting Partners
If one player dies, the other can revive them by pressing buttons quickly, ensuring both players remain engaged in the game.
